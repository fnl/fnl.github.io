About

Welcome, stranger! I am Florian "fnl" Leitner, an Austrian living in his exile of choice: Madrid, Spain. To give you a better idea of what you will find on this blog, here is a bit of a background about me.

Ever since I have started to study Molecular Biology, in particular focusing on Computational Biology for the last dozen or so years, I have worked as a Bioinformatician. If interpreted in the context of the current Big Data hype, I therefore am a "Data Scientist for Molecular Biology" - which indicates that we Bioinformaticans could very well be one of the most ancient Data Scientists around… My area of interest and PhD has been on Text Mining, obviously for Molecular Biology, and I have a healthy apetite for means that make machines "think" like humans, meaning Artificial Intelligence.

In general, I belive the declaration of the human rights to have been the biggest achievement of humanity so far (now we only need to actually follow them…) Also, I am a vivid defender of personal privacy and supporter of open source, while we urgently need to get rid of copyright laws and the patent system to not hamper our societal evolution. In terms of lifestyle, I love arts, travelling, nature, and Alpine sports. Finally, and most dear to my heart, is my just recently started family, that is my wife Mayte and son Alexander.

Over time, you will hopefully find some content about all these topics on this blog. For news and updates, feel free to follow me on Twitter (@flowing). If you wish to discuss anything with me, get hold of me via e-mail, please. Regarding my professional details, have a look at my CV.
